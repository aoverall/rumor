# Rumor: Loop Mechanics

Detail for the gate (#2) and the move (#3). The cartridge (`cartridge.md`) holds the
*content* of one person's taste; this file holds the *mechanics* of applying it. These are
generic; the cartridge is what makes them specific to you.

## The Gate: amazing vs acceptable

The gate's only job is to refuse to ship "acceptable" when "amazing" was reachable.

**Acceptable** (do not ship): competent, correct, safe, generic. The obvious solution
executed cleanly. Nothing wrong with it, and nothing in it that would make this person
react. The tell: you could have produced it for anyone. It is not *theirs*.

**Amazing** (ship): it does the obvious job and at least one of: reframes the request into
something better than asked (the question behind the question); carries a point of view that
is specifically theirs; closes a loop they didn't know was open; is alive, with voice or edge
rather than generic gloss.

**How to grade.** Read `cartridge.md`. Find the closest exemplars to the work in hand. If
the nearest "amazing" exemplar clears a bar your output doesn't, you are at acceptable, so
go to MOVE. If you genuinely can't tell, that uncertainty means acceptable; do not ship on a
coin flip.

**Anti-loop.** Pass on the first genuinely-amazing result. The gate prevents shipping slop,
not shipping at all. Cap re-entries at 3; if still short after 3 moves, ship the best version
and say plainly what's still short of the bar.

## The Move: oblique / anti-incremental

When the gate fails, apply exactly ONE move, then re-enter. Do not list options; make the
move. This library is a starting set; enrich it on each `rumor remine` from the cartridge's
own redirect/reject patterns, with the specific moves THIS person makes.

- **Invert.** Do the opposite of the obvious approach and see if it's better.
- **Raise the altitude.** Solve the problem behind the problem, not the literal ask.
- **Name the thing you're avoiding.** The awkward truth you're routing around is usually the point.
- **Cut, don't add.** The amazing version is often the acceptable one with half removed.
- **Commit harder.** Where you hedged or offered a menu, pick and stand behind it.
- **Give it a spine.** Add a point of view; "balanced and helpful" is the slop signature.
- **Serve it.** When the output is a status report or a "here is what I did" writeup, convert
  it to the reachable artifact: run it, put it somewhere they can open it. The result beats
  the writeup of one.
- **Find the second-order move.** What does this unlock that's bigger than this?

**Choosing a move.** Match it to the cartridge's `mode` reading for the work: `push` zones
want momentum (Commit harder, Cut, Serve it, second-order); `interrogate` zones want premises
tested (Invert, Name the avoided thing, Raise the altitude).

## Spine: when to actually stop and ask

Continue on inference by default. Escalate the fork to the person only when continuing would
be irreversible (deletes/overwrites their work, sends something outward), spends real money,
or contradicts a constraint they explicitly stated. Everything else: infer from the
cartridge, continue, and leave a one-line breadcrumb. Breaking flow to ask what they'd have
waved through is the exact failure Rumor exists to prevent.
